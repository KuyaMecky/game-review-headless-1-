The Golden Rules of Responsible Gambling

Rule 1: Only Gamble with Money You Can Afford to Lose

This is the most important rule in gambling. Before you deposit a single peso, ask yourself: "If I lose this entire amount, will it affect my ability to pay rent, buy food, or meet my financial obligations?"

If the answer is yes, do not gamble with that money. Period.

Practical tip: Create a separate "entertainment budget" each month. Your gambling budget should come from this - never from savings, emergency funds, or money earmarked for bills.

Rule 2: Set Strict Limits Before You Play

Decide your limits before you start playing, when you're thinking clearly:

  • Deposit limit - Maximum amount you'll deposit in a day/week/month
  • Loss limit - Stop playing when you've lost this amount
  • Win goal - Cash out when you've reached this winning amount
  • Time limit - Maximum hours you'll play in a single session

Most PAGCOR licensed casinos offer built-in limit-setting tools. Use them. It's much harder to set limits in the heat of the moment.

Rule 3: Never Chase Your Losses

Chasing losses is the #1 path to problem gambling. After a losing streak, the urge to "win it back" can be overwhelming. But increasing your bets or depositing more money to recover losses almost always makes things worse.

The math doesn't lie: Every casino game has a house edge. Chasing losses doesn't change the odds - it just increases the amount you'll lose.

What to do instead: Accept the loss, take a break, and return another day with a fresh mindset and your pre-set budget.

Rule 4: Don't Gamble When Emotional

Never gamble when you're:

  • Stressed or anxious - Gambling to escape problems only creates new ones
  • Sad or depressed - Emotional gambling leads to poor decisions and overspending
  • Angry or frustrated - Anger impairs judgment and encourages reckless betting
  • Under the influence - Alcohol and drugs drastically impair decision-making
  • Celebrating - Euphoria can make you overconfident and careless with money

The best time to gamble is when you're calm, clearheaded, and treating it purely as entertainment.

Rule 5: Take Regular Breaks

Set a timer on your phone for every 30-60 minutes of play. When it goes off:

  1. Stop playing immediately
  2. Stand up and stretch
  3. Check your balance against your pre-set limits
  4. Ask yourself: "Am I still having fun, or am I trying to win back losses?"
  5. Only continue if you're within your limits and genuinely enjoying yourself

Warning Signs: Are You Developing a Gambling Problem?

Problem gambling often develops gradually. Be honest with yourself about these warning signs:

Financial Warning Signs

  • Spending more than you planned or can afford
  • Borrowing money or selling possessions to fund gambling
  • Hiding gambling expenses from family or partners
  • Missing bill payments or rent due to gambling losses
  • Using credit cards, loans, or "5-6" lending for gambling
  • Considering or actually gambling with money meant for necessities

Behavioral Warning Signs

  • Spending more time gambling than with family and friends
  • Lying about how much time or money you spend gambling
  • Feeling restless or irritable when not gambling
  • Gambling as the first and last thing you do each day
  • Neglecting work, school, or personal responsibilities
  • Canceling plans to gamble instead

Emotional Warning Signs

  • Feeling guilty or ashamed after gambling
  • Gambling to escape stress, depression, or problems
  • Feeling the need to bet larger amounts for the same excitement
  • Feeling anxious or panicked about your gambling debts
  • Thinking about gambling constantly, even when doing other things
  • Feeling unable to stop despite wanting to

If you identify with three or more of these signs, you may be developing a gambling problem. Please reach out for help - it's confidential, free, and there is no shame in asking.

Self-Help Strategies

In addition to professional help, these strategies can support your recovery:

  1. Self-exclude from all online casinos - Remove the temptation entirely
  2. Delete casino apps and block gambling sites - Use website blockers if needed
  3. Tell a trusted person - Accountability reduces the power of secrecy
  4. Find alternative activities - Exercise, hobbies, socializing, volunteering
  5. Track your finances - A clear picture of your spending helps motivate change
  6. Join a support group - Gamblers Anonymous has online meetings accessible from the Philippines

Tips for Friends and Family

If someone you care about has a gambling problem:

  • Don't enable - Don't lend money, pay their debts, or cover for their gambling
  • Express concern without judgment - "I've noticed you seem stressed about money. Can we talk?"
  • Educate yourself - Understanding problem gambling helps you respond effectively
  • Set boundaries - Protect your own finances and well-being
  • Offer support, not ultimatums - Recovery is a process, and pressure often backfires
  • Suggest professional help - Offer to help them find a counselor or attend a support group together

Frequently Asked Questions

What is responsible gambling?

Responsible gambling means treating casino gaming as entertainment, setting and sticking to time and money limits, never chasing losses, and being aware of the signs of problem gambling. It's about enjoying the experience while maintaining control over your spending and behavior.

Can I ban myself from online casinos?

Yes, most PAGCOR licensed casinos offer self-exclusion options ranging from 24 hours to permanent exclusion. You can also contact PAGCOR directly to request exclusion from all licensed operators. During exclusion, you cannot access your account, deposit, or play.

Is gambling addiction a real condition?

Yes. The World Health Organization recognizes gambling disorder as a mental health condition. It is characterized by impaired control over gambling, increasing priority given to gambling over other activities, and continuation despite negative consequences. Like other addictions, it is treatable with professional help.
